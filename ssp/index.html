Summer Science Program (SSP)



I was selected for the 2025 Summer Science Program (SSP) in Astrophysics, a rigorous five-week residential research program. SSP is an international program that draws passionate minds from around the world, and I am so incredibly grateful to have had the chance to participate in this wonderful program.


Our project concerned the determination of a near-Earth asteroid's orbit. My group chose to determine the orbit of asteroid 7889 (1994 LX) in particular. We conducted observations using the University of Colorado Boulder's research-grade 20-inch PlaneWave telescopes, collecting spatial and photometric data on the asteroid. Using multivariable calculus and physics, we carried out the derivation of a method to determine the orbital elements and future position of an asteroid based on its initial celestial coordinates. Then, by implementing the method in Python, we applied it to our asteroid and successfully determined its orbit. Finally, we published our data to the International Astronomical Union's Minor Planet Center to help improve their models of the asteroid's trajectory.


SSP truly was an amazing experience. By providing the wonderful opportunity to be fully immersed in an incredible academic environment alongside my extraordinary peers, SSP has only deepened my intellectual curiosity and my love for astrophysics. Getting to be a part of this once-in-a-lifetime experience among one-of-a-kind people was something I'd never trade for the world.
